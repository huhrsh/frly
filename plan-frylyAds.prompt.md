fryly is a modern web app that acts as a shared home base for small, real-world groups who share everyday life together. It’s built primarily for ongoing groups like flatshares, families, close friend circles, and recurring crews who plan and do things together week after week—not for one-off, single-night events.

The core idea is simple: instead of juggling WhatsApp threads, random Google Docs, screenshots, and spreadsheets, each group gets one calm, structured workspace. Inside that workspace, everything important for that group lives in clearly labeled sections: lists, notes, reminders, calendar, photos, links, and shared expenses. Everyone in the group sees the same up-to-date information.

A typical user journey starts when someone creates a group for “Flatmates”, “Family”, or a recurring crew (like “Weekend rides” or “Trip gang”). The creator becomes the group admin and invites others via link or code. Once people join, they can see all the sections, add content, and keep things updated together.

Key user problems fryly is designed to solve:

- Plans and decisions getting lost in long chat histories.
- Household chores, bills, and groceries being tracked in ad-hoc ways that nobody fully trusts.
- Important documents and dates scattered across people’s devices and apps.
- Shared trip or group expenses that are hard to remember and settle fairly.
- A general sense of “we should be more organised” without wanting heavyweight project-management tools.

Core features and sections:

- Notes: Free-form pages for house rules, trip plans, packing lists, recipes, how-tos, and any shared information the group wants to write down. Everyone can view and edit so the “truth” lives in one place.
- Lists: Collaborative checklists for groceries, chores, errands, packing, and to-dos. Multiple people can check items off, reorder them, and keep track of what’s done.
- Reminders: Time-bound items like bill due dates, rent payments, renewals, birthdays, appointments, and chores. Reminders can send email notifications so important things don’t slip through the cracks.
- Calendar view: A shared calendar that shows events, trips, recurring tasks, and reminders in a visual format. It gives the group a simple timeline of what’s coming up.
- Gallery: A shared space for photos and images: trip pictures, receipts, scanned documents, and anything visual the group needs to keep together.
- Links and folders: Sections designed for saving useful links (e.g. rental agreement PDF, school portal, utility provider login page, travel bookings) and organising them into folders so they are easy to find later.
- Payments and expenses: Simple tools to record who paid for what, split bills between group members, and keep a running sense of who owes whom. It’s aimed at everyday shared costs like rent splits, utilities, groceries, dinner outings, and trip expenses.

Group structure and roles:

- Each user can belong to multiple groups (for example: one group for their flatshare, one for their family, one for a recurring friend crew).
- Group admins can create and rename sections, control who is in the group, and manage group settings.
- Regular members can view and contribute to sections depending on the group’s setup, so collaboration stays open while still having some structure and control.

Platform and experience:

- fryly is a web application with a responsive design that works well on phones, tablets, and desktops.
- It supports installation as a Progressive Web App (PWA), so users can “install” it to their home screen and use it like a lightweight app, with offline-friendly behaviour for cached pages and resources.
- The product aims for a calm, friendly, and minimal interface—no noisy timelines or notifications by default, just clear sections and content.
- It is designed to feel lighter and more personal than corporate tools like project-management platforms, but more structured and trustworthy than just relying on chat apps.

Positioning and target audience:

- Primary audience: people in shared living situations (flatmates, PGs, shared houses), nuclear and extended families, and small recurring social groups (friends who travel together regularly, ride together, or organise frequent events).
- Typical user is someone in their 20s to 40s who is “the organised one” in their group, tired of chasing information in chats, and looking for a simple place to put everything where others will actually see and use it.
- fryly is not targeted at large companies or formal teams with complex workflows, and it is not optimised for one-time events like a single night out. It is best when the group continues to exist over time and keeps coming back to the same shared space.

Value propositions:

- “One calm place for everything your group needs”: notes, lists, dates, files, photos, and expenses live together instead of being scattered.
- “Made for real life, not corporate projects”: appropriate for small, trusted groups that share a home or a recurring part of life.
- “Helps reduce friction and mental load”: fewer repeated questions (“What’s the Wi-Fi password?”, “Who paid the electricity bill?”, “When is that appointment?”) because the answers live in fryly.
- “Better than chat-only organisation”: chats remain for conversation; fryly becomes the stable, structured memory for the group.

Trust and safety assumptions:

- Users log in with email and password; passwords are sent over HTTPS and securely hashed on the backend.
- The app is multi-tenant: each group’s data is logically isolated from other groups in the database.
- Users can leave groups or be removed, and access is controlled at the group level.

Tone and brand:

- Friendly, approachable, and non-technical.
- Emphasises calm, reliability, and “home base” vibes rather than hustle, urgency, or corporate buzzwords.
- Messaging focuses on helping people feel more in control of the shared parts of their lives, with less confusion and fewer arguments over who forgot what.

You can treat fryly as: “a shared, always-there, lightweight workspace for the people you live and coordinate everyday life with—especially flats, families, and recurring friend groups—combining lists, notes, reminders, calendar, shared expenses, and photos into one simple app.”
